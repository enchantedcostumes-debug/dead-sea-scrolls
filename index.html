About This Project

What is this? A word-by-word mechanical translation of the Book of 1 Enoch from the original Ge'ez (Classical Ethiopic) language, preserved in Ethiopian manuscripts since antiquity.

Click any Ge'ez word to see its Fidel character breakdown, gematria value, root analysis, scholarly definition (Dillmann's Lexicon), and linguistic timeline from Proto-Semitic to modern Amharic.

Why Ge'ez? The Book of Enoch survives complete ONLY in Ge'ez. Greek and Aramaic fragments exist, but the Ethiopian Church preserved the full 108 chapters. This is the oldest complete witness to a text quoted in Jude 1:14-15.

Scholarly standard: Text follows R.H. Charles' 1906 critical edition of the Ethiopic text. English follows Charles' 1917 translation. Lexical data from August Dillmann's Lexicon Linguae Aethiopicae (1865) and Wolf Leslau's Comparative Dictionary of Ge'ez (2006).

All Sources Public Domain or Open Access
